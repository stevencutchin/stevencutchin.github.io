Motion Sensors: Accelerometer & Gyroscope

★ TRICORDER

Detects seismic activity, vibrations, and movement patterns. Scans for structural stability.

★ YOUR PHONE

Accelerometer measures linear acceleration on 3 axes. Gyroscope measures angular velocity (rotation). Together they track every tilt, shake, step, and gesture.

1. The Accelerometer

1.1 What It Measures

An accelerometer measures linear acceleration along three axes: X (left-right), Y (up-down), and Z (toward/away from the screen). When the phone is stationary, the accelerometer still reports data — it detects the constant pull of gravity at approximately 9.81 m/s². This is how the phone knows which way is “down” and can rotate the screen accordingly.

1.2 How It Works: MEMS

Modern phone accelerometers use MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) technology. Inside the chip is a microscopic mechanical structure — essentially a tiny mass suspended on silicon springs. When the phone accelerates, the mass deflects relative to the chip body. Capacitive plates on either side of the mass detect this deflection as a change in capacitance, which is converted to an electrical signal.

Figure 2.1 — Simplified MEMS accelerometer. A proof mass on springs deflects proportionally to acceleration. Capacitive plates measure the displacement.

The entire structure is microscopic — the proof mass is typically a few hundred micrometers across. Three of these structures, oriented perpendicular to each other, give the phone its 3-axis measurement capability.

1.3 Common Uses

Screen rotation (portrait/landscape), step counting (pedometer), shake detection, tilt-based games, fall detection (Apple Watch / newer iPhones), and vehicle motion detection in navigation apps.

2. The Gyroscope

2.1 What It Measures

A gyroscope measures angular velocity — how fast the phone is rotating around each of its three axes. While the accelerometer tells you which way is down, the gyroscope tells you how the phone is spinning. This is critical for smooth augmented reality, panoramic photography, and navigation systems that need to track heading changes.

2.2 How It Works: The Coriolis Effect

MEMS gyroscopes exploit the Coriolis effect. A tiny vibrating mass inside the chip moves back and forth along one axis. When the chip rotates, the Coriolis force pushes that vibrating mass in a perpendicular direction. The magnitude of this perpendicular deflection is proportional to the rotation rate.

Figure 2.2 — MEMS gyroscope principle. A mass vibrating along X experiences Coriolis force along Y when the chip rotates around Z.
Sensor Fusion: In practice, the accelerometer and gyroscope are always used together. The accelerometer drifts over time but gives an absolute “down” reference. The gyroscope is precise for short durations but drifts over time. Combining them (via a Kalman filter or complementary filter) gives you the best of both worlds — accurate, drift-free orientation tracking. This combination is often called an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU).

3. The Pedometer / Step Counter

Most modern phones include a dedicated low-power step-counting co-processor (Apple’s M-series motion coprocessor, or similar on Android). This chip continuously monitors accelerometer data to detect the rhythmic vertical bounce of walking — without keeping the main CPU awake. The result is always-on step counting that uses negligible battery.

4. As a Developer: Expo Sensors API

In React Native with Expo, you access motion sensors through the expo-sensors package:

import { Accelerometer, Gyroscope } from 'expo-sensors';

// Subscribe to accelerometer updates
Accelerometer.setUpdateInterval(100); // ms
const sub = Accelerometer.addListener(({ x, y, z }) => {
  console.log(`Accel: x=${x.toFixed(2)} y=${y.toFixed(2)} z=${z.toFixed(2)}`);
});

// Later: sub.remove() to unsubscribe
Battery Warning: Subscribing to sensor updates at high frequency (e.g., 16ms for 60fps) keeps the CPU active and drains battery rapidly. Always use the lowest update interval that your use case requires, and always unsubscribe when the component unmounts or the app goes to background.
