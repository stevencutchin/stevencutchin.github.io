The Mobile Tricorder: A Sensor-by-Sensor Guide to Your Phone

In 1966, the Star Trek Tricorder was pure science fiction — a handheld device that could scan, analyze, and sense the world around it. In 2026, the phone in your pocket does all of that and more. This series explores every sensor in a modern mobile device, how it works physically, and how you access it as a developer.

“It’s a Tricorder, Jim.”

A modern smartphone packs more sensing capability than any science fiction writer imagined. GPS triangulates your position on Earth to within meters. An accelerometer detects which way is down. A gyroscope tracks rotation. A barometer measures altitude. A magnetometer finds magnetic north. Cameras capture visible and infrared light. And radios connect to satellites, cell towers, WiFi, and nearby devices — all simultaneously.
