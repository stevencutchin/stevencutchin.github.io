DICTIONARY

Terms of the trade

Every term used across the fundamentals and method pages, in one place — grouped by the layer it belongs to: the NFT market, the Ethereum market, and the blockchain both run on.

NFT

the token market
NFT · token
A unique, non-fungible item recorded on chain. Identity is contract + token_id, fixed for life; it carries a pointer to its one current owner.
Collection
One smart contract defining a family of tokens (MOON, BAYC, AZUKI) and holding the ownership ledger for all of them. Named by its contract_address.
token_id
The number distinguishing one token inside its collection. Unique within the contract, meaningless outside it.
Owner · ownerOf
The address a token points to. Exactly one at any instant; only a trade or transfer moves the pointer.
Trade weight > 0
A priced sale: the token moves one way, ETH the other, between two addresses — one atomic swap with a price and timestamp.
Transfer weight = 0
An ownership move with no payment. Implies a shared hand or off-chain arrangement between the two addresses — which is why transfers seed candidate sets.
Ownership trace
One token's life: its time-ordered sequence of trades and transfers, each hop a (from, to, price, t) tuple. The unit step 1 collects.
Mint
A token's creation — the contract writes the first owner. The first hop of every ownership trace.
Marketplace
The venue where trades execute (OpenSea, Blur, LooksRare) via smart contracts. Anonymous by design: any address can trade with any other.
Wash trade
A trade whose buyer and seller are one hand — same entity or colluding group — creating volume and price without changing real ownership.

Ethereum

the currency market
Ether · ETH · Ξ
The native currency. Fungible — an amount with no identity, existing only as balances on addresses.
Wei
The smallest unit of ETH: 1018 wei = 1 Ξ. On-chain values are recorded in wei (value_wei).
Address
A wallet — the actor in both markets. It owns tokens, holds an ETH balance, and sends and receives transactions. Free to create in unlimited numbers.
Balance
An address's ETH holding: one running sum, the net of every transaction it has ever sent and received.
Normal transaction
A simple ETH transfer between two addresses, no smart contract involved: (from, to, value, t). Requires both parties' involvement, so it evidences a real relationship.
Ethereum transaction network
All normal transactions as one directed graph — addresses as nodes, transactions as edges, spanning the chain's entire history.
Linking graph derived
The connectivity graph built by BFS over the Ethereum transaction network from an initial set of addresses. Reveals off-market relationships between them. The output of step 2.
Gas
The fee paid to execute a transaction, priced in gwei (10⁹ wei). Every action on chain costs gas — including each leg of a wash cycle.
Exchange centralized
A custodial service (Binance, Coinbase) whose addresses transact with millions of users. Its edges reflect custody, not relationship.

Blockchain

the ledger underneath
Blockchain
A public, append-only ledger replicated across many machines. Everything above — tokens, balances, trades — is state recorded on it.
Block
A batch of transactions committed together, numbered in sequence (block_number). Blocks give the chain its ordering.
Timestamp
The time a block — and every transaction inside it — was committed. The t in every tuple; what makes traces time-ordered.
Transaction hash tx_hash
The unique fingerprint of one transaction. The permanent receipt: any recorded event can be looked up by it forever.
Smart contract
A program deployed at an address, executing automatically. NFT collections and marketplaces are smart contracts; a normal transaction involves none.
EOA externally owned account
An address controlled by a person's private key rather than by contract code. The wallets in traces and transactions are EOAs.
Keys public / private
The keypair behind an address: the public key derives the address; the private key signs its transactions. Whoever holds the key is the address.
Pseudonymity
Addresses carry no personal identity, and one person may hold many. The chain records addresses, not people — the gap wash trading hides in.
Immutability
Once committed, a record cannot be altered or deleted. Every trade, transfer, and transaction is permanent public evidence.
On-chain / off-chain
On-chain: recorded on the ledger, visible to analysis. Off-chain: agreements and identities the ledger never sees — inferable only from the on-chain shadows they cast.
