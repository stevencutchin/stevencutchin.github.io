Why the linking graph + traces reveal wash trading

A trade on the NFT market shows only that two addresses interacted there — nothing beyond it. An ETH transaction shows the same for its market. Neither alone is a relationship. But when the same two addresses appear in one token's trace and are connected on the Ethereum market, two independent interactions across separate systems point at the same pair — and that cross-market conjunction is proof of an existing relationship: they know each other, the precondition of a wash trade.

Why the cross-market connection is the proof

An interaction proves only itself

One trade, on either market, shows that two addresses met once — strangers do that constantly. Neither the NFT trade nor the ETH transaction, taken alone, says anything beyond the transaction it records.

The conjunction can't be coincidence

The two markets are independent systems — neither produces activity in the other. So the same pair appearing in both is not something either market's mechanics can generate: the recurrence comes from the people behind the addresses. That is an existing relationship.

Wash trading requires the relationship

A wash trade is by definition executed by related parties — one hand or a colluding group. And running one leaves cross-market tracks: sock-puppet wallets must be funded, and proceeds collected, on the very layer the linking graph reads.

One market's interaction proves nothing beyond itself. Recurrence across both markets proves the relationship — that is the signature the pipeline surfaces.
