Two files, one market

The NFT ledger is where the story is told; the ETH ledger is where the money moves. nft_trades_synth.csv records who owned which token when, and for how much. eth_market_synth.csv records the payments that make those trades real — plus everything around them: a shared funding wallet, an exchange hub, and organic traffic. Read either file alone and the picture is incomplete. Read them together and a three-wallet cabal becomes visible.

The two layers

Every priced NFT trade in the top file has exactly one settlement transaction in the bottom file: buyer pays seller the identical amount, one block later, with a nft_tx_hash back-reference. Those 365 legs are the plumb lines between the layers — they guarantee that all 43 NFT participants appear as vertices in the ETH graph, so a rendering of the ETH transactions shows the NFT market embedded inside it.

The custody trail is deliberately realistic: the hub pays the funder 16 ETH (one withdrawal, 400 blocks before trading opens), and the funder splits ~5 ETH to each of W0, W1, W2 within three blocks. Shared funding is the behavioral signal the pipeline is built to keep — it cannot be faked with dead addresses.

Connectivity: one graph with the hub, thirteen without it

With all 562 transactions present, the ETH graph is a single connected component — the hub touches 75 distinct counterparties and stitches every corner of the market together. That is exactly why the pipeline treats hubs as walls, not corridors. Wall the hub (sentinel group −1) and flood-fill the remainder, and the giant component dissolves:

One address becomes a singleton — its only counterparty was the hub itself. That is correct behavior, not an artifact: an address that transacts exclusively with an exchange has no peer relationships to analyze.

The degree knee

Degree here means distinct counterparties, never transaction count — a wallet that pings one partner 500 times is degree 1 and a signal to keep. On that metric the hub sits at 75 while the busiest honest trader reaches 21. The gap is a factor of 3.5, a clear knee rather than an orders-of-magnitude chasm — matching the power-law reality of Ethereum data rather than the convenient bimodal fiction.

Anatomy of a wash token

Three of the twelve tokens — 3401, 3405, 3409 — carry the cabal. Each follows the same five-phase lifecycle, shown here for token 3401 with its actual 33 rows:

The zero-value transfer is the entry ticket: it seeds a 2-set of suspects, per the rule that transfers seed, priced trades flag. The seed pairs rotate across the three tokens — (W0,W1), (W1,W2), (W2,W0) — so the per-token 2-sets merge into the full trio {W0, W1, W2} by shared membership, and the ETH layer's common funder independently corroborates the merge. The cycle pump then rotates ownership W0→W1→W2→W0 at a median gap of 3 blocks against 23 for organic trades, escalating price 3–12% per hop. The exit dump sells the inflated token to an organic buyer: the one trade in the pattern with a victim.

Cabal-authored rows total 78 of 368 (21%), but carry 210.4 of 733.8 ETH in priced volume — 29%. Volume concentration outrunning row concentration is itself a statistic worth teaching.
