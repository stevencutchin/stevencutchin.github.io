Website Privacy Checklist

Essential Items to Investigate for Privacy Compliance

TRAP AND TRACE (Cal. Penal Code ¬ß 638.51) - Addressing Information

Items that identify WHO is communicating or WHERE communications are directed (addressing/routing information)
  • IP address collection and transmission High Risk
  • Geolocation tracking (GPS, Wi-Fi, IP-based) High Risk
  • Unique identifiers (UUIDs, session IDs, user IDs) High Risk
  • Device information (hardware specs, GPU, screen resolution) Medium Risk
  • Browser information (type, version, plugins, extensions) Medium Risk
  • Age collection without consent Medium Risk
  • Gender collection without consent Medium Risk
  • Device fingerprinting (browser/device attributes) High Risk

WIRETAP (Cal. Penal Code ¬ß 631) - Content/Interaction

Items that capture the CONTENT of communications or user interactions with the website
  • Page views tracking Medium Risk
  • Content view tracking (what users read/watch) Medium Risk
  • Form interactions (typing without submission) Critical Risk
  • Form engagement tracking (focus, blur events) High Risk
  • Form submissions data capture High Risk
  • Search queries capture High Risk
  • Click tracking Medium Risk
  • Scrolling depth monitoring Medium Risk
  • Video engagement tracking (play, pause, seek) Medium Risk
  • Ad serving and tracking High Risk
  • Ad conversion tracking High Risk
  • Add to cart tracking Medium Risk
  • Checkout initiation tracking Medium Risk
  • Purchase/transaction tracking High Risk
  • Session duration monitoring Medium Risk
  • Session replay/recording Critical Risk
  • Keylogging (keystroke capture) Critical Risk
  • Mouse movement tracking High Risk

Third-Party Tracking Services

External services that receive user data from the website
  • Analytics platforms (Google Analytics, Adobe, etc.) High Risk
  • Advertising networks (Google Ads, DoubleClick, etc.) High Risk
  • Session replay tools (FullStory, Hotjar, LogRocket) Critical Risk
  • Social media pixels (Facebook Pixel, Twitter, LinkedIn) High Risk
  • Tag managers (Google Tag Manager, etc.) Medium Risk
  • Customer support tools (Intercom, Zendesk chat) Medium Risk
  • Marketing automation platforms (Marketo, HubSpot) High Risk
  • CDN and third-party libraries with tracking Medium Risk
  • Heatmap tools (Crazy Egg, Mouseflow) High Risk
  • A/B testing platforms (Optimizely, VWO) Medium Risk

Browser Storage & Fingerprinting

Technical methods for storing data and identifying devices
  • First-party cookies (site domain) Medium Risk
  • Third-party cookies (external domains) High Risk
  • Local Storage usage Medium Risk
  • Session Storage usage Medium Risk
  • IndexedDB usage Medium Risk
  • Canvas fingerprinting Critical Risk
  • WebGL fingerprinting Critical Risk
  • Audio fingerprinting Critical Risk
  • Font fingerprinting High Risk
  • Evercookies or respawning cookies Critical Risk

Privacy Compliance Requirements

Legal requirements and user rights that must be implemented
  • Privacy policy present and accessible Required
  • Privacy policy discloses all data collection Required
  • CCPA compliance section present Required CA
  • GDPR compliance for EU visitors Required EU
  • "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link (CCPA) Required CA
  • Opt-out mechanism functional Required
  • Cookie consent banner present Required
  • Cookie consent appears BEFORE tracking starts Critical
  • Granular consent options (accept/reject by category) Required
  • Consent choices respected (tracking stops if declined) Critical
  • Clear disclosure of third-party data sharing Required
  • Data retention policies disclosed Required
  • User data access mechanism (view my data) Required
  • User data deletion mechanism Required
  • Contact information for privacy requests Required

Security & Data Protection

Security measures to protect collected data
  • Cookies have Secure flag (HTTPS only) Required
  • Cookies have HttpOnly flag (prevent JS access) High Priority
  • Cookies have SameSite attribute (CSRF protection) High Priority
  • HTTPS enforced across entire site Required
  • Content Security Policy (CSP) implemented High Priority
  • Subresource Integrity (SRI) for third-party scripts Medium Priority
  • Data minimization practices (only collect what's needed) Required
  • Encryption for sensitive data transmission Required

Testing Methodology Notes

Essential steps for thorough privacy investigation
  • Test in clean VM or incognito mode for consistent results
  • Run Blacklight scan for automated tracker detection
  • Monitor DevTools Network tab during all interactions
  • Check DevTools Application tab for storage items
  • Test consent banner functionality (accept/reject/partial)
  • Verify tracking behavior before AND after consent
  • Document with timestamped screenshots
  • Save network HAR files for evidence
  • Review page source and JavaScript for hidden trackers
  • Test multiple user journeys (guest vs. authenticated)
  • Check for different behavior based on location/jurisdiction
